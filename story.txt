The Story of the Empty Cup

Long ago, in a quiet village, there lived a young man who was always restless. He chased after wealth, recognition, and possessions, yet he never felt satisfied. One day, he visited an old monk known for his wisdom.

The monk poured tea into the young man’s cup. He kept pouring until the tea spilled over, dripping onto the table. Shocked, the young man exclaimed, “Stop! The cup is full!”

The monk smiled gently and said, “Like this cup, your mind is overflowing—with desires, worries, and complaints. You cannot taste the tea of life until you empty it.”

The young man sat in silence. For the first time, he noticed the warmth of the tea, the fragrance of the leaves, and the kindness in the monk’s eyes. He realized that gratitude begins when we pause, empty our restless thoughts, and notice the simple gifts already present.

From that day forward, he practiced mindfulness each morning—listening to birdsong, feeling the sun on his skin, and giving thanks for the smallest blessings. His heart grew lighter, and he discovered that joy was not in chasing more, but in appreciating what already was.
